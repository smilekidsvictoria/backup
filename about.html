Welcome!

SMILEKIDS is an incorporated childcare facility that opened in 2020 and is licensed through the Ministry of Children and Family.

SMILEKIDS provides quality care, with ages ranging from infancy to pre-school in Victoria, B.C, Canada. Our priority is to provide a safe, and respectful environment.

We give our support in stimulating early childhood education.

About Us

SMILEKIDS programs are designed to meet the individual child's basic developmental needs. These needs include: social, physical, emotional, intellectual, creative, and self-concept, while also responding to families' needs. Children’s interactions will not only consist of peer interactions but also with staff who are trained to pick up on children’s needs. Themes are used to develop the child's knowledge of their world, this will be integrated within the learning activity Centre whenever possible.

SMILEKIDS will support and supplement parental care for children in a quality setting. The program supports all children's right, regardless of their diverse abilities, to participate actively in a natural environment with their community. Our Caregiving philosophy is based on respect. Each child's natural pace and level of development is supported. Each child is encouraged to learn about their world through play and become a self-learner. Communication, cooperation, and respect between educators and parents are considered essential. This ensures consistent care and consideration of each child and family's unique needs.

Curriculum

The program of SMILEKIDS childcare is play-based. Play-based learning is a type of early childhood education. This program focuses on children and how they interact with their surroundings through play. Children learn best while playing. Their learning ability is innate and develops through a variety of play experiences. Children's play begins early on, and their competence is developed through it. Play-based learning is helping children experience the content of the curriculum while showing the value of learning in an engaging form. The curriculum starts from observation and support of children's play.

Staff

We have qualified and professional staff including: a supervisor, Early Childhood Educators (ECEs), Infant and Toddler Educators (ITEs), Special Needs Educators, and Early Childhood Education Assistants (ECEAs) as specified by the Child Care Licensing Regulations. Our staff continues their education through workshops, conferences, and may take courses that will enhance their ability to support and educate children.
